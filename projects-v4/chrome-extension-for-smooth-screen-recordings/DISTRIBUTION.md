## 3. Validation Criteria

- **Continue building if:** Achieve 100 email sign-ups within the first week.
- **Pivot if:** Users express interest but highlight a major feature they feel is missing, indicating the product is on the right track but needs adjustment.
- **Kill if:** Fewer than 10 users engage with the product (comments, sign-ups, or inquiries) in the first week.

## 4. Week 2-4 Playbook

### If traction is working:
- **What to double down on:** Focus on engaging with users from LinkedIn and Product Hunt; create a weekly update post sharing user testimonials.
- **What content to create:** Write a blog post on "How to Create Professional Screen Recordings in Minutes" and share tips on using the extension effectively.
- **How to convert early users to paying customers:** Offer a limited-time discount for early adopters who provide feedback.
- **When and how to ask for feedback:** After one week of user engagement, send a follow-up email asking for feedback and suggestions for features.

### If traction is NOT working:
- **How to diagnose what's wrong:** Review comments and engagement metrics to identify if the problem lies with product features, messaging, or target audience.
- **What to try differently:** Adjust messaging to better highlight specific pain points and benefits based on user feedback; consider running a small ad campaign on LinkedIn targeting product managers.
- **When to officially kill it:** If no significant engagement or sign-ups occur within the first month, consider discontinuing the project or revisiting the product concept.
