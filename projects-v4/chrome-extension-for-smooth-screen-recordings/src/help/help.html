SmoothScreen Recorder

Getting Started

  1. Click the SmoothScreen icon in your Chrome toolbar to open the recorder popup.
  2. Adjust your settings before recording — set your preferred zoom level and cursor smoothness using the Settings page (gear icon in the popup footer).
  3. Click "Start Recording" and choose which screen, window, or tab you want to capture.
  4. Record your demo — SmoothScreen will automatically apply smooth cursor animations and natural zooms as you navigate.
  5. Click "Stop Recording" when you're done. Your recording will automatically download as a .webm file.

Frequently Asked Questions

Zoom Level controls how much the recording zooms in when your cursor stays focused on one area. At 100%, there's no zoom effect. At 200%, the view zooms to 2x magnification. The zoom transitions happen smoothly and automatically based on your cursor behavior.

Cursor Smoothness controls how much your raw mouse movement is smoothed out in the recording. A setting of 1 keeps your cursor movements nearly raw, while 10 applies heavy smoothing for silky-smooth cursor motion. Higher values introduce a slight delay but look much more polished.

Recordings are saved as .webm files (VP9 codec). This format is widely supported and offers excellent quality at reasonable file sizes. You can convert to MP4 using free tools like HandBrake or CloudConvert if needed.

If you see "Unable to start recording," check these things:

  • Make sure you grant screen capture permissions when prompted.
  • Check that no other application is using your screen capture.
  • Try reloading the page and clicking the extension icon again.
  • If your trial has expired, you'll need to upgrade to continue recording.

You get 7 days of full access to all recording features when you first install the extension. After the trial ends, you can upgrade for a one-time payment of $29.99 or subscribe at $4.99/month for additional cloud storage and advanced editing tools.

V1 focuses on video capture with cursor enhancement. Audio recording is planned for a future release.

Recordings are downloaded directly to your default Downloads folder. SmoothScreen does not store recordings on any server — everything stays on your machine. Cloud storage is available as a paid add-on in a future update.
