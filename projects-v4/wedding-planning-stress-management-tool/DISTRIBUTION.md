## 3. Validation Criteria

- **Continue building if:** 50+ unique visitors to the landing page and 10+ email sign-ups within the first week.
- **Pivot if:** Users express interest but mention a lack of specific features (e.g., cultural customization) or if feedback indicates the tool is not user-friendly.
- **Kill if:** Less than 10 unique visitors to the landing page and no email sign-ups within the first week.

## 4. Week 2-4 Playbook

### If traction is working:
- **What to double down on:** Continue engaging with communities that provided positive feedback and consider running a small contest for free access to the tool in exchange for testimonials.
- **What content to create:** Create blog posts or social media content addressing common wedding planning stressors and how your tool alleviates them.
- **How to convert early users to paying customers:** Offer a limited-time discount (e.g., $39 instead of $49) for early adopters who provide feedback.
- **When and how to ask for feedback:** After users have had a chance to use the tool for a week, send a follow-up email asking for their thoughts and suggestions.

### If traction is NOT working:
- **How to diagnose what's wrong:** Analyze user feedback for common themes; assess whether the product features align with user needs.
- **What to try differently:** Adjust the messaging to emphasize emotional support and community aspects of the tool, or consider adding features based on user feedback.
- **When to officially kill it:** If no significant traction (less than 5 email sign-ups) is seen by the end of Week 4, consider discontinuing the project.
